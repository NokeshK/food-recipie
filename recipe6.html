Homemade Sushi

Prep: 40 mins Cook: 20 mins (rice) Serves: 4-6

Fresh and Fun to Make

Making sushi at home can seem daunting, but it's a fun and rewarding process! This recipe covers the basics of making sushi rice and rolling simple maki rolls.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Sushi rice
  • 2.5 cups Water
  • 1/2 cup Rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Sugar
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 5-6 sheets Nori (seaweed)
  • Fillings Fresh fish (sushi grade), cucumber, avocado, crab sticks, etc.
  • For serving Soy sauce, wasabi, pickled ginger

Instructions

  1. 1

    Cook the Sushi Rice

    Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well and combine the rice and water in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, until all water is absorbed. Let it steam, covered, for 10 minutes off the heat.

  2. 2

    Season the Rice

    While the rice is steaming, gently heat the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small saucepan until the sugar and salt dissolve. Do not boil. Transfer the cooked rice to a large non-metallic bowl. Pour the vinegar mixture over the hot rice and gently fold it in with a wooden spoon or spatula until the rice is coated and glossy. Fan the rice while mixing to cool it down.

  3. 3

    Prepare Fillings

    Slice your chosen fillings into long, thin strips.

  4. 4

    Roll the Sushi

    Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat (makisu), shiny side down. Wet your hands slightly with water (to prevent sticking) and spread a thin, even layer of sushi rice over about 2/3 of the nori, leaving the top edge clear. Arrange your fillings in a line horizontally across the rice, about an inch from the bottom edge.

  5. 5

    Roll and Slice

    Using the bamboo mat, lift the edge closest to you and roll tightly, pressing gently to shape. Once rolled, moisten the top edge of the nori with a little water to seal the roll. Use a sharp, wet knife to slice the roll into 6-8 pieces.

  6. 6

    Serve

    Serve the sushi rolls with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

Tips & Notes

  • Use a very sharp knife dipped in water to slice the rolls cleanly.
  • Don't use too much rice; a thin layer is sufficient.
  • Experiment with different fillings to find your favorites.
