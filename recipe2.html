Homemade Pasta

Prep: 30 mins Cook: 10 mins Serves: 4

Fresh and Delicious

Making homemade pasta is a rewarding experience! It's surprisingly simple and the taste is far superior to dried pasta. All you need are a few basic ingredients and a little bit of time.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups All-purpose flour
  • 3 large Eggs
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • Optional A few drops of olive oil

Instructions

  1. 1

    Form the Dough

    On a clean surface or in a large bowl, pile the flour and make a well in the center. Crack the eggs into the well and add the salt (and olive oil if using). Use a fork to gently beat the eggs, gradually incorporating the flour from the sides.

  2. 2

    Knead the Dough

    Once a shaggy dough forms, use your hands to bring it together. Knead the dough for 10-15 minutes until it is smooth, elastic, and no longer sticky. If it's too sticky, add a little more flour; if it's too dry, add a tiny bit of water.

  3. 3

    Rest the Dough

    Wrap the dough tightly in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. This allows the gluten to relax, making the dough easier to roll.

  4. 4

    Roll and Cut

    Divide the dough into smaller portions. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one portion of dough very thinly using a rolling pin or a pasta machine. Cut the pasta into your desired shape (fettuccine, tagliatelle, etc.).

  5. 5

    Cook the Pasta

    Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add the fresh pasta and cook for 2-4 minutes, or until al dente. Fresh pasta cooks much faster than dried!

  6. 6

    Serve

    Drain the pasta and toss with your favorite sauce immediately.

Tips & Notes

  • Don't skip the resting step; it's crucial for easy rolling.
  • If you don't have a pasta machine, roll the dough as thinly as possible by hand.
  • You can dry fresh pasta by hanging it on a rack or dusting it with flour and coiling it into nests.
